Many people are too busy scrambling for food and survival to worry about the rest in that era. Under the impact of collectivization, China overturned the moral norms passed down from generation to generation, resulting in many abnormal behaviors. The National Bureau of Statistics of China (NBS)’s reports on Chinese women shows that there have been many positive changes in the status of Chinese women’s social status in the past 10 years.


First, the health status of women has improved significantly, and the reproductive health of rural women has remarkably improved. A notable change is that the percentage of rural women under the age of 35 who gave birth in hospitals has increased by 40.6 percentage points from a decade ago. The corresponding percentage change in urban areas is 9.7. This reflects the raising of women's fertility awareness and the improvement of medical system.



The concept of sharing housework is well accepted, and men are more involved in housework than before. 88.6% of the respondents agreed that "men should also take the initiative to undertake housework". Accordingly, the survey also shows that the time of housework of women in both urban and rural areas has decreased significantly.


Third, women are more actively participating in social activities and decision-making. There are more and more women performing as legislators, senior officials, and managers in a company. The proportion of women in non-governmental mutual aid and self-help organizations is higher than that of men.


The gender gap in education has impressively reduced. The mean years of education for women have an increase of 2.7 years compared with 2000,the gender gap narrowed to 0.3 years from the 1.5 years of a decade ago. And the proportion of women who have received high school and college education and above has increased significantly.



In addition, women's autonomy in deciding personal affairs has improved. 95% of women can satisfy their shopping desire and support their parents based on their opinoins. And men and women are more equal in making decisions on family affairs. Wives have a greater say in dome investment, loans, and other important decisions, and the proportion of this item is 14.3 percentage points higher than ten years ago.


Another noteworthy advancement is that the awareness of the Law on the Protection of Rights and Interests of Women has increased significantly. According to the survey, the number of people who know that China has this law has increased by 9.6 percentage points from a decade ago. All of these can illustrate that the social status of Chinese women has greatly improved compared with the past. After satisfying the basic needs of life, Chinese women's self-consciousness awakened and developed gradually, and they began to put forward the demand for more dignified lives and equal status.


Some people may wonder why Chinese women are not satisfied with so many things they already have and what the feminists are pursuing for now. Like the shadow under the sun, these rising satisfactory numerals cover so much unseen bitterness and sorrow. The social and cultural environment is not friendly enough towards Chinese women compared to men. Women are confronted with domestic violence, sexual exploitation, heavy housework, workplace harassment, gender discrimination, and many other endless problems. These social problems, like a knife that will fall on one's' head at any time, deeply troubled Chinese women.


In 2012, five Chinese feminists were arrested on a charge of picking quarrels and provoking trouble on the eve of Women's Day 2015. They once called for opposition to domestic violence, protested the inequality between men and women in Chinese society, and used performance art to fight for the rights of Chinese homosexuals. Their arrest was regarded as a turning point in Chinese feminism activism history. Hong Fincher acknowledged the importance of their actions to international solidarity in the fight for women’s rights.


However, on Chinese social media, feminists such as Zheng Churan, Li Maizi, and Lv Pin, are accused of generating gender antagonisms and hyping negative public opinion and are "in league with anti-China forces". Li Maizi, one of the feminist movement organizers, said: "China's political environment is unprecedentedly tense, and some things that were once legal are now illegal. Feminism has been politicized and stigmatized, causing the feminist movement to fall into a trough."

The World Gender Gap Report (WGGR) released by the World Economic Forum (WEF) shows that China ranks poorly in terms of gender equality level in the past 15 years. Last year, the Health and survival index even ranks in last place. What's worse, China's gender equality process has almost stagnated and even regressed. WEF believes that it will take another 100 years for China to build a gender-equitable society. Chen Zhi, a girl who actively discussed gender equality, said, "Life and health are the most fundamental human rights. Neither of them is adequately protected, let alone other rights and interests."


(Source: World Gender Gap Report)


What are the barriers to gender equality?

i. Legal Deficiency


On the eve of the founding of China, the "Common Program of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference" clearly stipulated that women have equal rights with men in politics. In addition, China has enacted several laws for women to provide exceptional protection for their rights and interests. For example, it stipulates the scope of labor work for female employees, guarantees the right of female employees to rest in particular periods, and ensures that women obtain the right to social security.


(Source:National laws and Regulations Database)

However, China's current legal system is not mature. On the one hand, when formulating laws, the legislature adhered to the legislative concept of "broad rather than detailed" and did not complete the details of some relevant regulations; on the other hand, the social development is too fast, and new problems emerge one after another. These laws previously formulated can no longer meet the needs of the new situation.


Sexual harassment in the workplace is nothing new. The law stipulates that employers are responsible for prohibiting sexual harassment, but the lack of details makes it still a severe problem. And women as vulnerable people are the main victims in the workplace. The survey showed that 66.5% of women had experienced sexual harassment in the workplace, and nearly 50% of women are told sex-related jokes during meals. In addition, from the perspective of existing laws, the ex-post remedies for sexual harassment are relatively limited. Victims are faced with problems such as high litigation costs, low trial efficiency, and difficulty in obtaining evidence. After winning the case, victims will receive very little financial compensation, and they may lose their jobs and suffer from mental health issues. In December 2018, a female employee sued her boss for sexual harassment. This case lasted for two years, and finally, the employee only received "a written or verbal public apology."

(Source:Report on sexual harassment in the workplace in China)


Another existing problem is that the law is not implemented in place and the relevant responsible people do not abide by the law. Article 23 of the Law on the Protection of Women's Rights and Interests: "When hiring female employees, all units shall not stipulate restrictions on the marriage and childbirth of female employees." According to employment data, nearly 90% of married women have been asked about their birth plans when applying for a job. Some interviewers require job applicants to guarantee "not getting married within two years" and "not having children within three years." 35% of bosses started hiring replacement candidates after they learned that their subordinates were pregnant. These employers have raised the employment standards of female job seekers and violated women's right to work and reproductive rights.

Labor Law and Employment Promotion Act stipulate it is illegal to refuse to employ women or raise the employment standards for women based on gender, except for the types of work or posts that are not suitable for women announced by the state. However, "Men only" job advertisements are common at multiple job fairs. In 2021, the National Civil Servant Examination recruited 9336 positions, of which 2130 positions preferred to hire men, accounting for 22.81%. And the research found that some civil service jobs are specified as "men only," "men preferred," or "suitable for men." In view of this situation, Mr. Yu, a human resources manager, believes, "The best way is to enhance your competitiveness and indicate your great value so that you can have more choices. When necessary, you should protect yourself by legal means."



In 2014, a girl named Mahu applied for a courier, and the courier company refused to hire a female courier because the courier needed to deliver heavy goods for a long time, citing the rules from Provisions on Labor Protection of Female Post and Telecommunications Workers. Mahu sued the company for "gender discrimination in employment" and won the lawsuit. These plausible protections for women have become restrictions on women's employment. The law and society should change the stereotype of women, and women should loosen the shackles of the traditional concept. Yi Yi, a lawyer, suggested that "when the law cannot take care of women as a disadvantaged party in every aspect, the only way is to make yourself strong so that women can suffer less unfairness.


Law and legal procedures always lag far behind social change. Any legislative revision is driven by public opinion. Everyone is the best spokesperson for their interests, and everyone must treat public affairs as their own. And Chinese law departments should speed up response to judicial needs and public expectations,and improve the chaos of lawlessness at all levels in China. The responsible subject should also establish rules and regulations to ensure that workers are in a safe working environment to achieve rapid and steady development.


ii.Political Censorship


Women's Day originated from the political events initiated by socialist feminists. At that time, poor working conditions and low wages made women workers respond to the call of equal rights activists, hold protests and demonstrations, and finally, they succeeded. The feminist movement in Western countries is an indispensable part of the pursuit of equal rights for men and women, and the activities of civil society are the main driving force to promote changes in legislation in a direction that is beneficial to women. Although similar social activities are rare in China, China has a tradition of feminist movement.


In the 1950s, a 22-year-old rural girl, Shen Jilan from Shanxi Province turned "equal wages for equal work" into a living fact in China. At that time, even in most developed countries, women worldwide could not even achieve basic "equal work," let alone "equal pay." In 1952, half a year after Shen Jilan successfully achieved equal work in the village, Prefectural Committee discovered her deeds in a coincidence. It was then turned into a gender equality movement promoted across the country, and more and more female industry representatives emerged. She is a well-deserved pioneer of Chinese feminism. Facts have also proved that Chinese women can make remarkable achievements under the correct leadership.


In China, at present, all kinds of feminist thoughts are in a state of chaos. Society requires women to actively fight for women's work, education, and autonomy, but it does not tell women what methods should be used to strive for it. Some feminists have learned Western experience and brought it to China to create a path with Chinese characteristics. However, when such a person appears, people will first analyze her political position and personal experience. Before they reached the achievement of advancing the equality process, these ideas were swamped by nationalism.


In March 2021, Xiao Meili posted that she dissuaded men sitting next to her from smoking and was splashed with hot oil in a restaurant, but the law enforcement department did not properly handle this matter. She exposed on the Internet to call for implementing a smoking ban and tobacco control in public places. And then, she launched a joint campaign of "Rejecting Second-hand Smoke" and "Against Gender Violence in Public Places".


But then she experienced the so-called political censorship of other KOLs and netizens against her. According to these two activities she carried out, she was questioned that she planed the entire inciden and attempted to vacillate over people's trust in the national system. When people really found her "Hong Kong independence statements", the whole incident developed from a "public health issue" to a "political issue."Soon, Xiao Meili's account was banned. "


After that, anyone who speaks for Xiao is subject to review and examination. Labels such as "Hong Kong Independence," "public knowledge of collecting money," and "conspiracy to divert attention" were tagged on Xiao's supporters. Admittedly, the political and ethnic problems is indisputable, but the good results of the Xiao Meili incident itself should not be ignored. Because of Xiao's event, the topic of tobacco control in public places has aroused people's attention and no smoking signs have been posted in some places.


After the "Five Sisters of Women's Rights" and some of their friends were banned, the feminist social movement in China fell silent. Many people preconceived that these activists were purposeful, but Lv Pin, a feminist activist, said, "They do not believe that the rise of a social movement can only be due to the people's aspirations." Wu Xiao, a sociologist, believes that international organizations that truly support women's rights and foreign forces that incite gender antagonisms both exist. "There are fewer campaigns in our country, and it is more likely that they are true for women's rights rather than for benefits."

The "fenimists representatives" with the highest recognition in China are often more moderate, outstanding people in various fields, such as Li Yinhe, Li Xiaojiang, Tu Youyou, and others. They naturally guide people forward like stars, and their stories subtly change society. However, the development of the Internet allows social news to spread more quickly. Under the pressure of gender-based violence and the pursuit of spiritual freedom, people's desire for social security has become urgent, and mild changes sometimes cannot relieve people's anxiety. Whether there are radical methods or gentle speeches, they should all have reason to speak up. More importantly, they both could promote social change and create a better living environment.


iii.Egoism and Consumerism


In Saudi Arabia, women will not be disturbed by unfamiliar men in public places and will be protected by male family members when they go out; Similarly, an Arab princess can enjoy a luxurious life without worrying about earning money. She even does not need to work, she can stay at home, and her father and brother will bear living expenses. It seems a paradise for women, but it does not manifest these Arabian countries' women's privilege. On the contrary, women are regarded as a vulnerable group in Saudi. Their male family member will influence their life trajectory. Since men provide all the material needs that women need, it is natural that this woman becomes an accessory to men. In other words, if women want to be respected and treated equally, they first need to be separate themselves from the identity of "male accessories". Arabian women are aware of this and have begun to seek the right to live free.




(Source:Love Tutor)


In 2017, the chairman of the Saudi Stock Exchange, the CEO of Samba Financial Group, and the CFO of Arab National Bank were successively held by women. Not only that, with the lifting of the female driving ban in 2018, female drivers have also become a new trend. According to Quartz, in 2019, 23% of Saudi women entered the labor market, which is a considerable improvement for Saudi Arabia compared with 18% ten years ago. At the same time, more and more people are beginning to understand and support women's work.

However, some Chinese KOLs advocate "female enjoyment," consumerism, and male hatred. They propose that men should spend money for women, buy gifts for girls during festivals and reshape women into roles that need to rely on men. It is natural for women to receive preferential treatment from men. Erich Fromm critically pointed out that modern production methods induce people to chase money madly and then fall into the quagmire of materialism in their daily lives and voluntarily give up freedom.


One typical example is the increasingly stringent "wedding tradition" in China. In recent years, the bride price increased higher and higher, the highest up to 300,000. Before marriage, the man needs to give the woman a bride price; later, the man has to afford the wedding expenses; he can also not marry the woman, but he will lose part or all of the bride price paid.Under this theory of materialized marriage, all factors such as age, health, appearance, knowledge, wealth, and ideas possessed by an individual are regarded as life assets. Bride-price tradition can be traced back to the ancient feudal society in China. The man paid the woman's parents; after that, she will take on family responsibilities, for example, looking after their parents-in-law, doing housework, and serving her husband.

With the progress of society and the liberation of human rights, women no longer have to bear social oppression. Since women have exempted the feudal society's obligation, they will lose their rights to a high bride price. Li Benji, a human rights research scholar, believes: "Most women shrink back from the actual obligations that accompany their rights, and they are not willing and do not dare to undertake these duties." They request gender equality, while unwilling to let go of a pampered life, then their determination to live on an equal footing is fake.

If women don't make efforts to change from themselves, the gender discrimination left over by history can't change automatically. Without quantitative change, people can never make a qualitative change. Modern women's rights are based on their economic self-determination and spiritual independence. Rights never depend on others for charity, but on oneself to fight for them. The equality of men and women means that duties and rights undertaken by men and women are equal. If a person only wants to enjoy his rights without performing his obligations, it's nothing less than self-destruction.

iv.Extremely Feminist Ideology

The male is still the most powerful group in China nowadays. Generally speaking, male violence against other groups happens the most and is the most serious. Men are usually the most favored in the workplace, and educational resources are also biased towards men.

The term feminism can be traced back to several equal rights movements in the last century. From women's demand for voting rights to broader civil rights, such as equal pay for similar work, equal education, and safer public space, the needs of each stage are different from the core of justice. It also overlapped with the racial equality movement and the LGBT movement in the middle and late last century.

However, "feminism" is interpreted by some people to be extremely radical and highly exclusive in modern China. When a feminist puts forward the term "feminism," it means that she will express her position on gender issues, including stereotyped influences, traditional concepts of gender roles, and so on. When wife killings and domestic violence continue to occur and have not yet been eliminated, Chinese women complain about the lack of extensive judicial assistance and social concern. The disappointment experienced by Chinese women under gender oppression is likely to be projected on specific male groups and even individuals. The report of Statista shows that 31% of men defined themselves as feminists. These men who do not have the will to oppress women may think this accusation is groundless. This emotional commonality and common hostility produce invisible groups, and then there is the phenomenon of group polarization, and the whole group becomes extremes. Women began to hate men, and men believed that women deserved it, and the two sides were in a deadlock.

Chen Jiming, a men who is concerned about gender equality, believes that it's just as shocking in most news related to direct violations of life and body, whether the victim is a man or a woman. Both women and relatively disadvantaged groups among men are affected by gender inequality . "The keypoint is not on gender, but on finding out why and how to solve it."

Due to the development of the Internet, women's awareness of equal rights has been repeatedly discussed on the Internet. However, apart from online platforms, many women in the real world are still in dire straits. Most people who advocate feminist rights online are fairly young women with knowledge and cultural literacy. They are only in Utopia and have not experienced cruel gender discrimination, said Yang Wenting. Under such a circumstance, the current Chinese feminist cannot unite women, and allies are therefore not available.

Among the 1,000 interviewees, 55% believe that the essential part of the future gender equality process should be women's performance in the political field.Many feminists who actively participate in gender issues on the Internet seem to be unaware that the slogans they put forward are incompatible with most women. Lacking allies and establishing numerous enemies, China's feminist rights are more like a weapon used by a small group of people to attack dissidents. Chinese feminists should prevent feminism from becoming a gimmick and deeply challenge vested interests. The feminist movement should push it to where it is needed more.

(Source:Statista,prepared by CHEN Wantong)


Although there are many differences within feminism, its purpose is to require women's liberation, independence, and equal rights with men. The wrong concept of feminism is the most significant resistance to the feminist movement in contemporary society, said Mu Qiaoyun. If women want to obtain absolute independence and equality, they must abandon the dualistic thinking mode. Women should emancipate their ideology and eliminate the opposition between the sexes. Women have always been a part of society, and women who want a better life cannot be separated from other groups. Women should tolerate all kinds of groups and ideas and find more allies to get support from all sides. Only when feminists achieve natural harmony between men and women will equal rights become true.

v. Expression Opportunity

After 1949, under the influence of Marxism, China called for the abolition of class emancipation and human rights, women were liberated as "oppressed" classes in this trend. Following the trend of the times, women participated in socially productive labor and gained independent income, thus launching a large-scale gender equality movement across the country. Therefore, the status of Chinese women has risen sharply.

At present, China's feminism is in a spontaneous rather than conscious stage, and it is under pressure counterattack driven by pain and stimuli. Women's rights were relatively insignificant in equal progress compared to the achievement made before liberation. Miss Jiao who works in the machinery industry said: "Women's status has been greatly improved compared to before, but most people still have gender discrimination. Although gender equality has been advocated in our country for years, I think the effect is minimal." As for what needs to be achieved in the next stage of gender equality, modern China does not seem to have an accurate goal.

Modern social media provides a direction for women. It is a significant development that the rise of social media has made it easier for individuals who are originally isolated and helpless to gain public support through social media. At the same time, the female-led consumption model is deeply changing the business model, “Her Economic” comes into being. Chinese media cater to the thinking methods of independent women to express, which has indeed alleviated the pressure on women in real society to a great extent. These business promotions have established a lot of independent and self-reliant images of women and created a relatively friendly environment for women on the Internet. For example, an advertisement released by Johnson & Johnson in 2019 describes three mothers of different times. That modern mother is a medical worker. After receiving the anti-epidemic task, she devotes herself to the place where she is more needed, to realize her personal value.

(Srouce:MarkLives)


These femvertisings help women express themselves on the Internet. However, the nature of commercial is not to comfort women in virtual posts, but profit-seeking. It cannot bring about substantive changes to gender equality. Chen Hao analyzes postmodern feminism based on the theory of empowerment. For women to truly gain more rights, it is not enough to gain the right to speak. What is more important is women's own knowledge of themselves, and their thinking on philosophical propositions such as "who am I" and "what do I want to do".

It's much harder to say what you want than just saying "no". Because if you imform what you want, you will give people a lot of opportunities to refute you and restrict you. It seems that you have lost a lot of power. However, determining, claiming, and pursuing what you want, are part of the real essence of rights, as defined by Wesley Newcomb Hohfeld (1913).

It is inevitable to discuss the standard behavioral norms. Any group that wants to strive for a common goal as a whole must have a joint program of action. It is necessary to be clear about what can and cannot be done. In this way, people will not use their rights unscrupulously and will not lose their way in the fight.

Looking back on the history of Western feminist development, it is inseparable from the joint efforts of groups. The first women's scientific research society was founded in the Netherlands in 1785. In the reform movement of the 19th century. In 1848, the first feminist conference was held in Seneca Falls, New York. In 1903, Emmeline Pankhurst established the Women's Social and Political Union, intending to expose the institutionalized gender discrimination in British society.

However, Chinese society seems not to give feminists such an opportunity to gather together. In 2018, the well-known feminist self-media in China, "Feminist Voices", was permanently banned. In 2021, some feminist accounts have slowly disappeared on Chinese social media. The New York Times reported that at least 15 famous Chinese feminist accounts were closed due to public tip-offs.

Lv Pin, the founder of "Feminist Voices", said that, in China, feminists are easily attacked as they speak. The reason is that when feminists put forward structural explanations for gender inequality or domestic violence, the explanation will make many people feel violated.Banned feminists say that in a country where the government controls the Internet and suppresses social movements, depriving feminists of their speech freedom on the Internet is an increasingly strengthened part.

Those actively seeking women's healthy development are silenced, and the awakened who are in a confusion stage will inevitably be extremely conservative under the fear of social events. Some people put forward "6B4T" to protect themselves. The core of all contradictions has become female safety, and there is no confrontation for the real enemy of women. Various schools of thought will emerge in the development of social ideology and culture, but we should be vigilant when there is only one voice left. "When society only allows one voice to exist, then that voice is a lie." China should give feminists more opportunities to elaborate so that people can understand the spiritual core of feminism and independently think about the right and wrong of feminism.

(Source:"6B4T")

Looking back over the past 50 years, the quality of life of Chinese women has benefited from the improvement of the economic level of the whole country and women's participation in the labor market. Although there are still many deficiencies compared with western countries, the emergence of outstanding women in all walks proves that the status of Chinese women will be higher and higher. Wu Xiao is hopeful about future development. She said, "It's progress that feminism, which was previously inconspicuous, is now beginning to gain social attention. Nowadays, women's awareness of independence is becoming stronger and stronger, which is also a manifestation of progress. Perhaps the progress is slow now, but when our generation becomes the main force of society, the road of gender equality will not be so difficult."

Advocating gender equality is very important to all aspects of a healthy society. According to Amartya Sen's theory, development is a process in which all people expand all kinds of freedoms equally, and gender equality itself is a core development goal. Gender equality is everyone's business, and everyone needs to strive for it. If we can understand and respect each other, an equal future will eventually come true.
