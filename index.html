The National Bureau of Statistics of China (NBS)’s reports on Chinese women shows that there have been many positive changes in the status of Chinese women’s social status in the past 10 years.


First, the health status of women has improved significantly, and the reproductive health of rural women has remarkably improved. A notable change is that the percentage of rural women under the age of 35 who gave birth in hospitals has increased by 40.6 percentage points from a decade ago. The corresponding percentage change in urban areas is 9.7. This reflects the raising of women's fertility awareness and the improvement of medical system.



The concept of sharing housework is well accepted, and men are more involved in housework than before. 88.6% of the respondents agreed that "men should also take the initiative to undertake housework". Accordingly, the survey also shows that the time of housework of women in both urban and rural areas has decreased significantly.


Third, women are more actively participating in social activities and decision-making. There are more and more women performing as legislators, senior officials, and managers in a company. The proportion of women in non-governmental mutual aid and self-help organizations is higher than that of men.


The gender gap in education has impressively reduced. The mean years of education for women have an increase of 2.7 years compared with 2000,the gender gap narrowed to 0.3 years from the 1.5 years of a decade ago. And the proportion of women who have received high school and college education and above has increased significantly.



In addition, women's autonomy in deciding personal affairs has improved. 95% of women can satisfy their shopping desire and support their parents based on their opinoins. And men and women are more equal in making decisions on family affairs. Wives have a greater say in dome investment, loans, and other important decisions, and the proportion of this item is 14.3 percentage points higher than ten years ago.


Another noteworthy advancement is that the awareness of the Law on the Protection of Rights and Interests of Women has increased significantly. According to the survey, the number of people who know that China has this law has increased by 9.6 percentage points from a decade ago. All of these can illustrate that the social status of Chinese women has greatly improved compared with the past. After satisfying the basic needs of life, Chinese women's self-consciousness awakened and developed gradually, and they began to put forward the demand for more dignified lives and equal status.


Some people may wonder why Chinese women are not satisfied with so many things they already have and what the feminists are pursuing for now. Like the shadow under the sun, these rising satisfactory numerals cover so much unseen bitterness and sorrow. The social and cultural environment is not friendly enough towards Chinese women compared to men. Women are confronted with domestic violence, sexual exploitation, heavy housework, workplace harassment, gender discrimination, and many other endless problems. These social problems, like a knife that will fall on one's' head at any time, deeply troubled Chinese women.


In 2012, five Chinese feminists were arrested on a charge of picking quarrels and provoking trouble on the eve of Women's Day 2015. They once called for opposition to domestic violence, protested the inequality between men and women in Chinese society, and used performance art to fight for the rights of Chinese homosexuals. Their arrest was regarded as a turning point in Chinese feminism activism history. Hong Fincher acknowledged the importance of their actions to international solidarity in the fight for women’s rights.


However, on Chinese social media, feminists such as Zheng Churan, Li Maizi, and Lv Pin, are accused of generating gender antagonisms and hyping negative public opinion and are "in league with anti-China forces". Li Maizi, one of the feminist movement organizers, said: "China's political environment is unprecedentedly tense, and some things that were once legal are now illegal. Feminism has been politicized and stigmatized, causing the feminist movement to fall into a trough."

The World Gender Gap Report (WGGR) released by the World Economic Forum (WEF) shows that China ranks poorly in terms of gender equality level in the past 15 years. Last year, the Health and survival index even ranks in last place. What's worse, China's gender equality process has almost stagnated and even regressed. WEF believes that it will take another 100 years for China to build a gender-equitable society. Chen Zhi, a girl who actively discussed gender equality, said, "Life and health are the most fundamental human rights. Neither of them is adequately protected, let alone other rights and interests."

"You can't get anything without paying"

In Saudi Arabia, women will not be disturbed by unfamiliar men in public places and will be protected by male family members when they go out; Similarly, an Arab princess can enjoy a luxurious life without worrying about earning money. She even does not need to work, she can stay at home, and her father and brother will bear living expenses. It seems a paradise for women, but it does not manifest these Arabian countries' women's privilege. On the contrary, women are regarded as a vulnerable group in Saudi. In other words, if women want to be respected and treated equally, they first need to be separate themselves from the identity of "male accessories". Arabian women are aware of this and have begun to seek the right to live free.




In 2017, the chairman of the Saudi Stock Exchange, the CEO of Samba Financial Group, and the CFO of Arab National Bank were successively held by women. According to Quartz, in 2019, 23% of Saudi women entered the labor market, which is a considerable improvement for Saudi Arabia compared with 18% ten years ago. At the same time, more and more people are beginning to understand and support women's work.

However, some Chinese KOLs advocate "female enjoyment," consumerism, and male hatred. They propose that men should spend money for women, buy gifts for girls during festivals and reshape women into roles that need to rely on men. It is natural for women to receive preferential treatment from men. Erich Fromm (1956) critically pointed out that alienation is everywhere in modern society, and the development of capitalism makes people fall into the trap of commodity fetishism.


One typical example is the increasingly stringent "wedding tradition" in China. In recent years, the bride price increased higher and higher, the highest up to 300,000. Before marriage, the man needs to give the woman a bride price; later, the man has to afford the wedding expenses; he can also not marry the woman, but he will lose part or all of the bride price paid.Under this theory of materialized marriage, all factors such as age, health, appearance, knowledge, wealth, and ideas possessed by an individual are regarded as life assets. Bride-price tradition can be traced back to the ancient feudal society in China. The man paid the woman's parents; after that, she will take on family responsibilities, for example, looking after their parents-in-law, doing housework, and serving her husband.

With the progress of society and the liberation of human rights, women no longer have to bear social oppression. Since women have exempted the feudal society's obligation, they will lose their rights to a high bride price. Li Benji, a human rights research scholar, believes: "Most women shrink back from the actual obligations that accompany their rights, and they are not willing and do not dare to undertake these duties." They request gender equality, while unwilling to let go of a pampered life, then their determination to live on an equal footing is fake.

If women don't make efforts to change from themselves, the gender discrimination left over by history can't change automatically. Without quantitative change, people can never make a qualitative change. Modern women's rights are based on their economic self-determination and spiritual independence. Rights never depend on others for charity, but on oneself to fight for them. The equality of men and women means that duties and rights undertaken by men and women are equal. If a person only wants to enjoy his rights without performing his obligations, it's nothing less than self-destruction.

After 1949, under the influence of Marxism, China called for the abolition of class emancipation and human rights, women were liberated as "oppressed" classes in this trend. Following the trend of the times, women participated in socially productive labor and gained independent income, thus launching a large-scale gender equality movement across the country. Therefore, the status of Chinese women has risen sharply.

At present, China's feminism is in a spontaneous rather than conscious stage, and it is under pressure counterattack driven by pain and stimuli. Women's rights were relatively insignificant in equal progress compared to the achievement made before liberation. Miss Jiao from Hubei said: "Women's status has been greatly improved compared to before, but most people still have gender discrimination. Although gender equality has been advocated in our country for years, I think the effect is minimal." As for what needs to be achieved in the next stage of gender equality, modern China does not seem to have an accurate goal.

Modern social media provides a direction for women. It is a significant development that the rise of social media has made it easier for individuals who are originally isolated and helpless to gain public support through social media. At the same time, the female-led consumption model is deeply changing the business model, “Her Economic” comes into being. Chinese media cater to the thinking methods of independent women to express, which has indeed alleviated the pressure on women in real society to a great extent. These business promotions have established a lot of independent and self-reliant images of women and created a relatively friendly environment for women on the Internet. For example, an advertisement released by Johnson & Johnson in 2019 describes three mothers of different times. That modern mother is a medical worker. After receiving the anti-epidemic task, she devotes herself to the place where she is more needed, to realize her personal value.

These femvertisings help women express themselves on the Internet. However, the nature of commercial is not to comfort women in virtual posts, but profit-seeking. It cannot bring about substantive changes to gender equality. Chen Hao (2018) analyzes postmodern feminism based on the theory of empowerment. For women to truly gain more rights, it is not enough to gain the right to speak. What is more important is women's own knowledge of themselves, and their thinking on philosophical propositions such as "who am I" and "what do I want to do".

It's much harder to say what you want than just saying "no". Because if you imform what you want, you will give people a lot of opportunities to refute you and restrict you. It is inevitable to discuss the standard behavioral norms. Any group that wants to strive for a common goal as a whole must have a joint program of action. Looking back on the history of Western feminist development, it is inseparable from the joint efforts of groups. The first women's scientific research society was founded in the Netherlands in 1785. "When society only allows one voice to exist, then that voice is a lie." China should give feminists more opportunities to elaborate so that people can understand the spiritual core of feminism and independently think about the right and wrong of feminism.

Right and wrong of feminist leaders

Women's Day originated from the political events initiated by socialist feminists. At that time, poor working conditions and low wages made women workers respond to the call of equal rights activists, hold protests and demonstrations, and finally, they succeeded. Women’s activities in civil society are the main driving force for the change of legislation in a direction that is beneficial to women. In the 1950s, a 22-year-old rural girl, Shen Jilan from Shanxi Province turned "equal wages for equal work" into a living fact in China. At that time, even in most developed countries, women worldwide could not even achieve basic "equal work," let alone "equal pay."


In 1952, half a year after Shen successfully achieved equal work in the village, Prefectural Committee discovered her deeds in a coincidence. It made a report, which was turned into a gender equality movement promoted across the country, and more and more female industry representatives emerged. She is a well-deserved pioneer of Chinese feminism. Facts have also proved that Chinese women can make remarkable achievements under the correct leadership.


In China, at present, all kinds of feminist thoughts are in a state of chaos. These ideologies mutually catalyze, encourage, oppose, and quarrel with each other, and they move towards a vicious circle. Society requires women to actively fight for women's work, education, and autonomy, but it does not tell women what methods should be used to strive for it. Some feminists have learned Western experience and brought it to China to create a path with Chinese characteristics. "Gender equality is everyone’s business."

The male is still the most powerful group in China nowadays. Generally speaking, male violence against other groups happens the most and is the most serious. Men are usually the most favored in the workplace, and educational resources are also biased towards men.

The term feminism can be traced back to several equal rights movements in the last century. From women's demand for voting rights to broader civil rights, such as equal pay for similar work, equal education, and safer public space, the needs of each stage are different from the core of justice. It also overlapped with the racial equality movement and the LGBT movement in the middle and late last century.

However, "feminism" is interpreted by some people to be extremely radical and highly exclusive in mordern China. When a feminist puts forward the term "feminism," it means that she will express her position on gender issues, including stereotyped influences, traditional concepts of gender roles, and so on. When wife killings and domestic violence continue to occur and have not yet been cut off, Chinese women complain about the lack of extensive judicial assistance and social concern. The disappointment experienced by Chinese women under gender oppression is likely to be projected on specific male groups and even individuals. The report of Statista shows that 31% of men defined themselves as feminists. These men who do not have the will to oppress women may think this accusation is groundless. This emotional commonality and common hostility produce invisible groups, and then there is the phenomenon of group polarization, and the whole group becomes extremes. Women began to hate men, and men believed that women deserved it, and the two sides were in a deadlock.

Chen Jiming, a male student, believes that it's just as shocking in most news related to direct violations of life and body, whether the victim is a man or a woman. Both women and relatively disadvantaged groups among men are affected by gender inequality . It is more important to find out why it happened and solve it.

Due to the development of the Internet, women's awareness of equal rights has been repeatedly discussed on the Internet. However, apart from online platforms, many women in the real world are still in dire straits. Most people who advocate feminist rights online are fairly young women with knowledge and cultural literacy. They are only in Utopia and have not experienced cruel gender discrimination, said Yang Wenting (2021). Under such a circumstance, the current Chinese feminist cannot unite women, and allies are therefore not available.

Miss Jiao appreciates the way women unite and fight for women's rights and interests bit by bit, starting from small things. "Fierce sports are not desirable. Now in a society ruled by law, I don't think it has reached the point where it is irretrievable." Among the 1,000 interviewees, 55% believe that the essential part of the future gender equality process should be women's performance in the political field.Many feminists who actively participate in gender issues on the Internet seem to be unaware that the affirmative slogans they put forward are incompatible with most women. Lacking allies and establishing numerous enemies, China's feminist rights are more like a weapon used by a small group of people to attack dissidents. Chinese feminists should prevent feminism from becoming a gimmick and deeply challenge vested interests. The feminist movement should push it to where it is needed more.

Although there are many differences within feminism, its purpose is to require women's liberation, independence, and equal rights with men. The wrong concept of feminism is the most significant resistance to the feminist movement in contemporary society, said Mu Qiaoyun (2020). If women want to obtain absolute independence and equality, they must abandon the dualistic thinking mode. Women should emancipate their ideology and eliminate the opposition between the sexes. Women have always been a part of society, and women who want a better life cannot be separated from other groups. Women should tolerate all kinds of groups and ideas and find more allies to get support from all sides. Only when feminists achieve natural harmony between men and women will equal rights become true.

Looking back over the past 50 years, the quality of life of Chinese women has benefited from the improvement of the economic level of the whole country and women's participation in the labor market. Although there are still many deficiencies compared with western countries, the emergence of outstanding women in all walks proves that the status of Chinese women will be higher and higher. Women's equal rights consciousness is about women getting a better life in modern society, being generally respected, and avoiding unnecessary losses because of gender. It is not the idea that women should have a higher status than men, and women should be more respected than men. Women's rights are not about women's higher status but equality between men and women. If we can mutually understand and respect, the future of equality will eventually be realized.
