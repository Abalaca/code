The National Bureau of Statistics of China (NBS)’s reports on Chinese women shows that there have been many positive changes in the status of Chinese women’s social status in the past 10 years.


First, the health status of women has improved significantly, and the reproductive health of rural women has remarkably improved. A notable change is that the percentage of rural women under the age of 35 who gave birth in hospitals has increased by 40.6 percentage points from a decade ago. The corresponding percentage change in urban areas is 9.7. This reflects the raising of women's fertility awareness and the improvement of medical system.



The concept of sharing housework is well accepted, and men are more involved in housework than before. 88.6% of the respondents agreed that "men should also take the initiative to undertake housework". Accordingly, the survey also shows that the time of housework of women in both urban and rural areas has decreased significantly.


Third, women are more actively participating in social activities and decision-making. There are more and more women performing as legislators, senior officials, and managers in a company. The proportion of women in non-governmental mutual aid and self-help organizations is higher than that of men.


The gender gap in education has impressively reduced. The mean years of education for women have an increase of 2.7 years compared with 2000,the gender gap narrowed to 0.3 years from the 1.5 years of a decade ago. And the proportion of women who have received high school and college education and above has increased significantly.



In addition, women's autonomy in deciding personal affairs has improved. 95% of women can satisfy their shopping desire and support their parents based on their opinoins. And men and women are more equal in making decisions on family affairs. Wives have a greater say in dome investment, loans, and other important decisions, and the proportion of this item is 14.3 percentage points higher than ten years ago.


Another noteworthy advancement is that the awareness of the Law on the Protection of Rights and Interests of Women has increased significantly. According to the survey, the number of people who know that China has this law has increased by 9.6 percentage points from a decade ago. All of these can illustrate that the social status of Chinese women has greatly improved compared with the past. After satisfying the basic needs of life, Chinese women's self-consciousness awakened and developed gradually, and they began to put forward the demand for more dignified lives and equal status.


Some people may wonder why Chinese women are not satisfied with so many things they already have and what the feminists are pursuing for now. Like the shadow under the sun, these rising satisfactory numerals cover so much unseen bitterness and sorrow. The social and cultural environment is not friendly enough towards Chinese women compared to men. Women are confronted with domestic violence, sexual exploitation, heavy housework, workplace harassment, gender discrimination, and many other endless problems. These social problems, like a chopper that will fall on ones' head at any time, have deeply disturbed Chinese women.


In 2012, five Chinese feminists were arrested on a charge of picking quarrels and provoking trouble on the eve of Women's Day 2015. They once called for opposition to domestic violence, protested the inequality between men and women in Chinese society, and used performance art to fight for the rights of Chinese homosexuals. Their arrest was regarded as a turning point in Chinese feminism activism history. Hong Fincher acknowledgedthe importance of their actions to international solidarity in the fight for women’s rights.


However, on Chinese social media, feminists such as Zheng Churan, Li Maizi, and Lv Pin, are accused of generating gender antagonisms and hyping negative public opinion and are "in league with anti-China forces". Li Maizi, one of the feminist movement organizers, said: "China's political environment is unprecedentedly tense, and some things that were once legal are now illegal. Feminism has been politicized and stigmatized, causing the feminist movement to fall into a trough."

The World Gender Gap Report (WGGR) released by the World Economic Forum (WEF) shows that China ranks poorly in terms of gender equality level in the past 15 years. What's worse, China's gender equality process has almost stagnated and even regressed, and China needs another 100 years to build up a real gender equality society.


(Source: World Gender Gap Report, prepared by CHEN Wantong)

The gap between law and reality

On the eve of the founding of China, the “Common Program of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference” clearly stipulated that woman have equal rights with men in politics. After that, China has provided many protection laws for women.


In addition, it has also made special protection for women's rights and interests. For example, it stipulates the scope of emergency work for female employees, guarantees the right of female employees to rest in special periods, and ensures that women obtain the right to social insurance.


(Source:National laws and Regulations Database,prepared by CHEN Wantong)

Although many legal regulations protect the enjoyment of women's rights in real life, the rights that women should enjoy equally often encounter conflicts with traditional customs and practices.


"Labor Law" and "Employment Promotion Act" stipulate it is illegal to refuse to employ women or raise the employment standards for women based on gender, except for the types of work or posts that are not suitable for women announced by the state. However, at various job fairs, "men only" job advertisements can be found everywhere. In 2018, the National Civil Servant Examination recruited 16,144 positions, of which 4162 positions preferred to hire men, accounting for 25.78%.


According to the recording till 2017, of the 204 members of the 19th CPC Central Committee, only 10 are women; of 172 alternate members, only 20 female members. Furthermore, among the 25 members of the Politburo, only one female, and there are no women among the seven members of the Standing Committee.


In view of this situation, Mr. Yu, a human resources manager, believes that "the best way is to enhance your competitiveness and indicate your great value so that you can have more choices. When necessary, you should protect yourself by legal means."


Article 23 of the Law on the Protection of Women's Rights and Interests: "When hiring female employees, all units shall not stipulate restrictions on the marriage and childbirth of female employees." Nevertheless, some interviewers require job applicants to make guarantees of "not getting married within two years" and "not having children within three years".


These employers obviously have raised the employment standards of female job seekers and violated women's right to work and reproductive rights.


Chinese law clarifies that employers should prohibit sexual harassment. However, these laws and regulations do not stipulate the responsibilities of employers, which also leads to the lack of preventive measures against sexual harassment in the workplace, and most women who are sexually harassed bear it silently.


The reality in China is still very heavy. To narrow the gap between the picture of legal planning and real life, and to improve the chaos of lawlessness at all levels in China has always been a problem that Chinese law departments are thinking about. Feng Yuan, a woman rights worker who is committed to promoting the implementation of China’s Anti-domestic Violence Law, said: "if the legal system itself is not well established and there are no other supporting systems, accountability is relatively difficult. Without accountability, this is the case. "


However, according to the bill proposed at this year's National People's Congress, at present, Chinese legislators focus more on economic development and clean governance. Although there are laws and regulations issued in response to current social events, it is still difficult to implement the laws in place. Lawyer Gao Hao said that "there is a shortage of police, prosecutors, judges, and lawyers, but there is no lack of violations" .


Yi Yi, a lawyer, suggested that "when the law is not able to take care of women as a disadvantaged party in every aspect, the only way is to make yourself strong so that women can suffer less unfairness. "


Do's and Don's

At present, China's feminism is actually in a stage of spontaneous rather than self-conscious, a stressful counterattack driven by pain and stimulation. Women's rights after liberation were quite insignificant in terms of legislative progress compared to the achievement made before liberation.

After 1949, the sharp rise in the status of Chinese women was not on account of what women have done, but a top-down political policy. This policy was determined by Marxism, which called for the elimination of class liberation human rights, and women were liberated as "oppressed" classes in this trend. Women have improved their quality of life without making too much effort. As a result, women, later on, do not know what they should do.

It is an important development that the rise of social media has made it easier for individuals who were originally isolated and helpless to gain public support through social media. At the same time, Chinese media cater to the way of thinking of independent women to express, which has indeed alleviated the pressure on women in real society to a great extent. However, the nature of commercial is not to comfort women in virtual posts, but profit-seeking. These business promotions have established a lot of independent and self-reliant images of women, and also create a relatively friendly environment for women on the Internet.

Chen Hao analyzes postmodern feminism based on the theory of empowerment. For women to truly gain more rights, it is not enough to gain the right to speak. What is more important is women's own knowledge of themselves, and their thinking on philosophical propositions such as "who am I" and "what do I want to do".

Summarizing all the "dons" cannot be equated to what you have declared what you want. It's much harder to say what you want than just saying "no". Because if you say what you want, you will give people a lot of opportunities to refute you and restrict you. It seems that you have lost a lot of power. However, even so, determining, claiming, and pursuing what you want is still part of the real essence of rights, as defined by Hohfeld N.W. (1903).

The emergence of a clear program will limit the possibility of women's rights doing whatever they want. It is inevitable to discuss the common behavioral norms. Any group that wants to strive for a common goal as a whole must have a common program of action. Such a program cannot only include “don's" and "oppose” but must also include goals other than objections. And this goal must logically regulate the common behavior of the organization members based on whether it is conducive to the realization of the goal, and the emergence of a clear program will limit women's rights and ensure that it will not act willfully.

Looking back on the history of Western feminist development, it is inseparable from the joint efforts of groups. The first women's scientific research society was founded in the Netherlands in 1785. In the reform movement of the 19th century. In 1848, the first feminist conference was held in Seneca Falls, New York. In 1903, Emmeline Pankhurst established the Women's Social and Political Union, intending to expose the institutionalized gender discrimination in British society.

However, Chinese society seems not to give feminists such an opportunity to gather together. In 2018, the well-known feminist self-media in China, "Feminist Voices", was permanently banned. In 2021, some feminist accounts have slowly disappeared on Chinese social media. The New York Times reported that at least 15 famous Chinese feminist accounts were closed due to public tip-offs.

Lv Pin, a Chinese feminist activist and founder of "Feminist Voices", said in an interview with Voice of America (VOA) that, in China, feminists are easily attacked as they speak. The reason is that when feminists put forward structural explanations for gender inequality or domestic violence, the explanation will make many people feel violated.

Banned feminists say that in a country where the government controls the Internet and suppresses social movements, depriving feminists of their speech freedom on the Internet is an increasingly strengthened part.

Those who actively seek the healthy development of women are silenced, while those who advocated consumerism using the slogan of "feminists" attracted many believers. China should give feminists more opportunities to elaborate. Only by understanding more thoughts, awakened ones who are in a confused stage can realize the spiritual core of feminism and independently think about the right and wrong of feminism.

Right and wrong of feminist leaders

In 2020, Shen Jilan, the initiator of equal wages for equal work, passed away suddenly.


In the 1950s, women all over the world, even in the most developed countries, could not even achieve basic "equal work", let alone "equal pay". Unbelievably, a 22-year-old rural girl from Shanxi Province turned "equal wages for equal work" into a living fact for the first time in China in 1951.


In the countryside at that time, without any reference materials, Shen had to overcome many deep-rooted prejudices and difficulties. She mobilized women in the co-operative to work in the fields with men. On the one hand, women faced physical inconvenience, on the other hand, working with men is regarded as a disgraceful thing at that moment. However, Shen came over many obstacles and achieved the goal of "equal work".


Western feminists later fought for "equal pay for equal work" for more than half a century. Only two years after China's liberation, and just after the end of the millennium marriage system, it was brought into reality under Shen Jilan's efforts.


In 1952, half a year after Shen successfully achieved equal work in the village, Prefectural Committee discovered her deeds in a coincidence and made a report, which was turned into a gender equality movement promoted across the country, and more and more female industry representatives emerged. She is a well-deserved pioneer of Chinese feminism. Facts have also proved that under the correct leadership, Chinese women can make remarkable achievements.


In China, at present, all kinds of feminist thoughts are in a state of chaos. These ideologies mutually catalyze and encourage, oppose and quarrel with each other, and each of them is moving towards a vicious circle. Women urgently need feminist leaders in the new century.


However, on the one hand, Chinese society seeks the next leader, on the other hand, it does not allow a radical leader. In this contradictory situation, women's rights fall into a dilemma. Society requires feminists to strive for women's right to work, education, and autonomy. It is to help women better assume social responsibilities and accept more exercises. Nevertheless, when such a person appears, people will make a demanding analysis of her political position and personal experience.


In March 2021, Xiao Meili posted that she dissuaded men sitting next to her from smoking and was splashed with hot oil in a restaurant, but this matter was not properly handled by the law enforcement department. She appealed to the Internet to call for the implementation of smoking ban and tobacco control in public places.





But then she experienced the so-called political censorship of other KOLs and netizens against her, and based on the evidence of her statement about Hong Kong independence, questioned that she planed the entire incident. Her attempt was to vacillate over people's trust in the national system. Soon, Xiaomei's account was banned. "


The fermentation of subsequent events is more like another gender violence in public spaces. Anyone who speaks for Xiao is subject to review and examination. Labels such as "Hong Kong Independence", "public knowledge of collecting money" and "conspiracy to divert attention" were tagged on Xiao's supporters, and then people who claimed that patriotism was above all else spared no effort to condemn and suppress their targets, and they advised these reactionaries to be silenced and banned.


But because of Xiao's event, people started to think about tobacco control in public spaces, and she initiated a joint campaign for comprehensive tobacco control. If women's rights pay such a painful price every step forward, it may curb people's determination to pursue justice for women. Lv Pin said that because China's Internet continues to block feminist organizations, it is impossible to find a long-lasting public space to make a voice. Therefore, they have to run around finding space to maintain.

The consciousness of nationalism has become the strongest protection of patriarchal actions in China's cyberspace, and nationalism as the highest power discourse instantly replaces the original proposition. They do not believe that the rise of a social movement can only be due to the aspirations of the people, Lv Pin analyzed.

And the value system of this group is not "the legitimacy of the state." Anything that shakes its legitimacy is "spreading harmful information."

The underlying logic is that Chinese people should have only one group value system to serve the masses, and the solution of problems in various internal dimensions would be ignored and sacrificed. The value system of this group must be "the legitimacy of the country", and anything that shakes its legitimacy is "spreading harmful information".

Gender issues in public were dissatisfied with its original sin of "gender", and finally received blockade for unwarranted crime in the name of nationalism, which finally triggered a mass vent and "carnival". Lv Pin said, in this preposterous era, the only crime we commit is the crime of feminism.

The government firmly controls the direction of public opinion in the media and oppresses the Chinese feminist movement repeatedly and as a result, Chinese feminists are relatively radical, and feminism develops slowly.


Allies and enemies

The Male is still the most powerful group in China nowadays. Generally speaking, male violence against other groups happens the most and is the most serious. Men are usually the most favored in the workplace, and educational resources are also biased towards men.

In 2020, when wife killings and domestic violence continue to occur and have not yet been cut off, in the views of most feminists, feminist rights are not completely equal to "equal rights". The term "equal rights" that includes "male rights" and "feminist rights" appears to be less popular.

The term feminism can be traced back to several equal rights movements in the last century. From women's demand for voting rights to broader civil rights, such as equal pay for equal work, equal education, and safer public space, the demands of each stage are different from the core of justice. It also overlapped with the racial equality movement and the LGBT equality movement in the middle and late last century.

When a feminist puts forward the term "feminism", it means that she will express her position on gender issues, including stereotyped influences, traditional concepts of gender roles, and so on. It also means that she will specifically care about gender topics, with corresponding critical tools. Some radical feminist rights will emphasize "female" and adopt extreme isolationism towards other genders.

These people will emphasize the identity of the biological sex and believe that physiological gender is a gap that hinders people's mutual understanding, and different genders can never cross it. Such aggressive gender opposition cognition has led to confrontation and mutual distrust between men and women. Chinese women complain about the lack of extensive social union foundation, practical system, and judicial assistance. The disappointment experienced by Chinese women under gender oppression is likely to be projected on specific male groups and even individuals. And then, these men who do not have the will to oppress women may think this accusation is groundless.

What can unite a group is often the common enemy. Therefore, this emotional commonality and common hostility produce invisible groups and form an invisible program to restrict the behavior of all members. Then there is the phenomenon of group polarization, and the whole group becomes extremes. Thus, reflexive anti-feminism was formed. At this time, anti-feminism is not only addressing some uncomfortable statements but a thing that must be done in order to maintain their group existence. The individual will is affected to some extent by the invisible collective will, therefore, feminists themselves created enemies. The report of Statista shows that 31% of men defined themselves as feminists. Since this is the case, do not push away this part of the masses and make them become the enemy of women.

Some feminist activists who appeared online tarnish the reputation of feminism. Due to the development of the Internet, women’s awareness of equal rights has been repeatedly discussed on the Internet. However, apart from online platforms, a considerable number of women in the real world are still in dire straits. Most of the people who advocate feminist rights online are fairly young women with knowledge and cultural literacy. They are only in Utopia and have not experienced cruel gender discrimination, said Yang Wenting. Under such a circumstance, the current Chinese feminist cannot way to unite women, and allies are therefore not available.

Chinese feminists should prevent feminism from becoming an artifice of empty talk, and challenge those with vested interests more deeply. And the feminist movement should get rid of the shackles of the interests of the middle class and push it to places where it is more urgently needed. At least it can be asserted that the breaking of the old system must come from an objective examination of the world, society, and even itself.

Although there are many differences within feminism, its purpose is to require women's liberation, independence, and equal rights with men. In contemporary society, the wrong concept of feminism is the biggest resistance to the feminist movement, said Mu Qiaoyun. If women want to obtain real independence and equality, they must abandon the dualistic thinking mode. Women should not only emancipate their ideology but also eliminate the opposition between the sexes. Only when feminists achieve real harmony between men and women, will equal rights become true.

Looking back over the past 50 years, the quality of life of Chinese women has benefited from the improvement of the economic level of the whole country. Although there are still many deficiencies compared with western countries, the emergence of outstanding women in all walks proves that the status of Chinese women will be higher and higher. Women's equal rights consciousness is about women getting a better life in modern society, being generally respected, and avoiding undue losses because of gender. It is not the idea that women should have a higher status than men, and women should be more respected than men. Women’s rights are not about women’s higher status, but for equality between men and women. If each of us can mutually understand and respect, the future of equality will eventually be realized.
